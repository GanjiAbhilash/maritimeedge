Mundra & Kandla Ports Digitise CFS Documentation, Cut Container Dwell Time

Mundra Kandla Customs April 2026

April 2026 witnessed several procedural and facilitative measures across Gujarat Customs covering major gateways such as Mundra, Kandla (Deendayal Port), Hazira, Pipavav, and connected CFSs and ICDs. The focus remains on trade facilitation, digitisation, and congestion management at India's largest EXIM hub.

Infrastructure & Terminal Operations

Customs and port authorities continued efforts to improve cargo evacuation, streamline terminal operations, and reduce dwell time through closer coordination with terminal operators, CFSs, and transporters.

CFS & ICD Operational Measures

Operational advisories emphasized:

  • Timely documentation submission
  • Optimized gate operations
  • Compliance with Customs examination and scanning procedures at CFSs and ICDs

Digital & Compliance Enhancements

April 2026 updates reinforced the use of electronic systems for documentation, access control, and auditability to ensure transparency and faster customs clearances across Gujarat ports.

Trade Facilitation Initiatives

Trade facilitation remained a priority with measures focused on:

  • Reducing transaction costs for exporters and importers
  • Improving predictability of customs processes
  • Enabling faster cargo release for compliant trade

Stakeholder Advisory

Importers, exporters, customs brokers, and logistics operators are advised to stay aligned with local customs advisories and port-specific SOPs to avoid delays and non-compliance. The April 2026 Customs updates reflect continued emphasis on operational efficiency, digital compliance, and trade facilitation. Proactive alignment will help stakeholders maintain smooth cargo flows across ports, CFSs, and ICDs in the Gujarat region.
